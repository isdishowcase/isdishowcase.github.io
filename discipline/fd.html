Vishwa

Masked Moss

Through my thesis, I speculated the environmental and emotional needs of humans to survive in and till twenty seventy. Global warming, global soil degradation and solastalgia will be the greatest challenges mankind will face in the coming years. To battle these grave challenges and stall their claws from deeply rooting on this planet and uprooting life from it we need to take action to change the trajectory of our doomed future. Fashion industry and its fast paced production and consumption are the major stakeholders of these walls closing in on us with nowhere else to go.
Holistically envisioned circular designs made with consciously chosen materials in sync with the climatic conditions expected in 2070 is what I aimed to achieve, and did rightfully so. Algae, Hemp, Lotus and Charcoal. I developed cocktails of superpowers through varied fibre blends of these four materials. These materials fit into the revised slow systems that are employed to revive indigenous communities’ craft and regenerate agriculture and fashion.
The communities production reinforce the preservation of a traditional, natural system existing in our culture for centuries. These regenerative steps will slow the cycle of consumption, increase resource productivity while increasing the emotional value of the product, with minimum wastage of resources, making this a model of a circular economy.
By applying these methods right from tomorrow we might change the scenario currently expected for 2070. Let’s use indigenous knowledge with technological advancements of today to get the best of both worlds and regenerate handspun, handwoven, hand knitted fashion as a trend that will save us from our doomed future.
